Centers X(503)-X(510), and also centers X(173), X(174), X(258), and X(351)-X(364) are associated with isoscelizers.

<p>A line L<sub>A</sub> perpendicular to the internal bisector
        line of A is an <i>A-isoscelizer.</i>. Suppose X is a point not on a
        sideline of ABC, and let</p>


<table>
<tr><td>L(A,X) =</td><td>the A-isoscelizer passing through X;</td>
</tr>
		<tr><td>E(A,X) =</td><td>L(A,X)&cap;AC;</td></tr>
		<tr><td>F(A,X) =</td><td>L(A,X)&cap;AB;</td></tr>
		<tr><td>T(A,X) =</td><td>the triangle wth vertices A, E(A,X), F(A,X);</td></tr>
		<tr><td>H(A,X) =</td><td>A-altitude of T(A,X);</td></tr>
		<tr><td>D(A,X) =</td><td>distance between E(A,X) and F(A,X);</td></tr>
		<tr><td>X(A) =</td><td>distance between E(A,X) and F(A,X);</td></tr>
</table><br>
	<p>Cyclically define L(B,X), E(B,X), . . . , X(B) and L(C,X), E(C,X), .
        . . , X(C).</p>
<p>Each center, X(503) to X(510), is defined by Peter Yff as the point
        X of concurrence of isoscelers satisfying certain conditions.</p>
