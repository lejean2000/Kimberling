In this short note we gather some definitions of operations which preserve triangle centrality.

# Operations Involving Two Triangles 

Below we will consider the operations on two triangles, ABC and A'B'C' which in some cases may be degenerate (a line or a point).  

The following four operations are symmetric.

## Vertex triangle  
The triangle formed by lines AA', BB', and CC'.

## Side triangle  
The triangle with vertices BC ∩ B'C', CA ∩ C'A', AB ∩ A'B'.

## Cross triangle
The triangle with vertices BC' ∩ B'C, CA' ∩ C'A, AB' ∩ A'B.

## Mid triangle
The triangle with vertices the midpoints of AA', BB', and CC'.  

## Perpendicular-bisector triangle
The triangle formed by the perpedicular bisectors of AA', BB', and CC'. Decomposable to Orthoidal(Mid(ABC,A'B'C') wrt Vertex(ABC,A'B'C'))
  
The operations defined next are not symmetric.

## Orthoidal triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C'
The triangle formed by the lines through A, B, C perpendicular to B'C', C'A', A'B', respectively.

## Paraidal triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C'
The triangle formed by the lines through A, B, C parallel to B'C', C'A', A'B', respectively.

## Antisymmetric triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C'
The triangle formed by the reflections of A in A', B in B', and C in C'. It is accepatable to have ABC or A'B'C' degenerate into a point.

## Side reflection triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C'
The triangle formed by the reflections of lines BC, CA, AB in lines B'C', C'A', A'B', respectively.

---

## Side cross-reflection triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C'
$A_{1}B_{1}C_{1}$, the side cross-reflection triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C' is defined as follows.  
Let:  
$L_{AB}$ = the reflection of line BC in line A'B'  
$L_{AC}$ = the reflection of line BC in line A'C'.  
  
Cyclically define $L_{BA}$, $L_{BC}$, $L_{CA}$, $L_{CB}$ and:  
$A_{1}= L_{AB} ∩ L_{AC}$  
$B_{1}= L_{BA} ∩ L_{BC}$  
$C_{1}= L_{CA} ∩ L_{CB}$  

## Vertex-side reflection triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C'
The triangle formed by the reflections of A, B, and C in lines B'C', C'A', A'B', respectively. It is accepatable to have ABC degenerate into a point.

## Cross-tripolar triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C'
The triangle formed by the tripolars of B'C', C'A', A'B' wrt ABC.

## Projection triangle of ABC wrt A'B'C'
The triangle formed by the projections of A, B, C upon B'C', C'A', A'B' resp. 
If ABC degenerates to a point P, then the projection triangle coincides with the pedal triangle of P wrt A'B'C'.



# Operations Involving Three Triangles 

Below we will consider some operations involving three triangles, ABC and A'B'C', and XaXbXc.

## {XaXbXc, ABC, A'B'C'}-1st-cross triangle
Denote Pa = XaA∩B'C'. Cyclically define Pb, Pc.
We call PaPbPc the {XaXbXc, ABC, A'B'C'}-1st-cross triangle.
{X, ABC, ABC}-1st-cross triangle is the cevian triangle of X wrt ABC.

## {XaXbXc, ABC, A'B'C'}-2nd-cross triangle
Denote Pab = XaA∩A'B', Pac=XaA∩A'C',  Qab = XaA'∩AB, Qac=XaA'∩AC. 
Let Ta=PabQac∩PacQab and cyclically define Tb and Tc.
We call TaTbTc the {XaXbXc, ABC, A'B'C'}-2nd-cross triangle.

## {XaXbXc, ABC, A'B'C'}-3rd-cross triangle
Denote Pab = XaA∩A'B', Pac=XaA∩A'C',  Qab = XaA'∩AB, Qac=XaA'∩AC. 
Let Ta=PabQab∩PacQac and cyclically define Tb and Tc.
We call TaTbTc the {XaXbXc, ABC, A'B'C'}-3rd-cross triangle.
