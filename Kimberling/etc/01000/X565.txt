<h3 id="X565">X(565)&nbsp;=&nbsp;INTERSECTION OF LINES X(49)X(93) AND  X(143,324)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; cos(3B - 3C) : cos(3C - 3A) : cos(3A - 3B)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;sin A cos(3B - 3C) : sin B cos(3C - 3A) : sin C cos(3A - 3B)
<p>
X(565) lies on these lines: 49,93&nbsp;&nbsp; 143,324</p>

<p>
Centers X(566)-X(584) lie on the Brocard axis, L(3,6). Each is the center X of a circle that meets the sides of triangle ABC with three equal angles at X.
</p><p>
Let A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> denote the meeting-points; e.g., A<sub>B</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> are on side CA. The equal angles are given by
</p><p>
D = angle(A<sub>B</sub>XA<sub>C</sub>) = angle(B<sub>C</sub>XB<sub>A</sub>) = angle(C<sub>A</sub>XC<sub>B)</sub>
</p><p>
Then trilinears for X are given by
</p><p>
X = sin A + cot D/2 cos A : sin B + cot D/2 cos B : sin C + cot D/2 cos C.
</p><p>
Definitions: Y is the <b>orthogonal</b> of X if D(X) + D(Y) = &pi;/2;
</p><p>
Y is the <b>harmonic</b> of X if X and Y are harmonic conjugates with respect to X(3) and X(6);
</p><p>
Y is the <b>orthoharmonic</b> if Y is the harmonic of the orthogonal of X. The centers in this section were contributed by Edward Brisse, December, 2000.
</p>
